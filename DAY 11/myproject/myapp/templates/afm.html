AFM

subject that focuses on equipping individuals with the knowledge and skills to handle complex strategic financial decisions in organizations. It builds upon the foundational concepts of Financial Management and delves into more advanced areas like capital markets, risk management, and corporate finance. AFM is typically offered as an elective or optional paper in professional accounting and finance qualifications such as ACCA and CA Final. 
Key areas covered in AFM:
Advanced Concepts in Financial Management:
This includes topics like financial policy and corporate strategy, risk management, and advanced capital budgeting decisions. 
Capital Markets and Instruments:
AFM explores various aspects of capital markets, including different financial instruments and their pricing. 
Risk and Return:
The subject covers the relationship between risk and return, along with security pricing and analysis. 
Derivatives:
AFM delves into currency and interest rate derivatives, their uses, and how they impact financial decisions. 
Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) and Valuation:
This area focuses on the financial aspects of M&A transactions, including valuation techniques. 
Working Capital Management:
AFM also covers the management of a company's short-term assets and liabilities.
